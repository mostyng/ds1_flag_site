Symbolizing the hope, how the people who lives in Providence in the past and currently have tried to follow the city’s motto, and how can the future generations could also respect the motto, and continuing pass the idea to the next generation.

Kids tend to write a picture on the condensation window. I remember when I was small, drawing many things with my friends on the window, and one of them was our dreams. I believe that I am not the only ones who did this, and many kids in the past, and in the future, will do the similar thing. And people who done a great job for example in baseball, awarded players will stamp their hand print in the ground, and sign. This is to let the people in the future to remember the excellent player. These two facts let me lead to this flag idea.

– David Iwanaga

FLAGS.RISD.GD

There are four unused flagpoles on the Providence River outside RISD’s Design Center. First-year students in RISD GD have proposed flags to symbolize one of the following concepts: community, divergent or shared histories, civic pride, change/growth, hope or something else. We hope these designs will create a conversation with the public in lieu of or in advance of printed flags. Learn more.
